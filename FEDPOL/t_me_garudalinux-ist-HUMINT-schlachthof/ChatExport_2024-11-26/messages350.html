Token: The smallest unit of a text, such as a word, punctuation mark, or subword.

Tokenization: The process of splitting text into tokens.

Stemming: The process of reducing words to their base or root form, e.g., "walking" becomes "walk".

Lemmatization: Similar to stemming, but considers the correct grammatical root, e.g., "went" becomes "go".

Part-of-Speech Tagging (POS-Tagging): Assigning parts of speech (noun, verb, etc.) to each token in a sentence.

Named Entity Recognition (NER): The process of identifying named entities in text, such as people, organizations, or places.

Dependency Parsing: Analyzing the grammatical structure of a sentence to identify relationships between words.

Collocation: Frequently co-occurring words that form a particular meaning, e.g., "strong tea".

Collocation Analysis: Studying word pairs or groups that commonly appear together.

N-Gram: A sequence of n consecutive tokens or words in a text. A 2-gram (bigram) would be "strong tea".

Word Embeddings: Vector representations of words in a continuous space that capture semantic similarities between words, e.g., Word2Vec, GloVe.

Bag of Words (BoW): A model representing text by the frequency of words without considering word order.

TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency): A statistical measure that evaluates the importance of a word in a document relative to its frequency in a corpus.

Sentiment Analysis: The detection and classification of the emotional tone of texts, e.g., whether a text is positive, negative, or neutral.

Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA): A technique for uncovering hidden meaning structures in text by analyzing word distribution across documents.

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA): An algorithm for topic modeling that tries to discover topics within a corpus of documents.

Word Sense Disambiguation (WSD): The process of determining the correct sense or meaning of an ambiguous word in a given context.

Coreference Resolution: Identifying which words or phrases in a text refer to the same entity.

Sentence Segmentation: The process of dividing a text into individual sentences.

Language Model: A model that predicts the probability of a sequence of words, e.g., GPT, BERT.

Transformer: A neural network model that uses self-attention to model relationships between words, regardless of their distance in the text (e.g., BERT, GPT).

Attention Mechanism: A technique used in sequence processing to highlight the most relevant parts of the input.

Corpus: A large and structured set of texts used for training and evaluating NLP models. It can consist of documents, books, websites, etc.
