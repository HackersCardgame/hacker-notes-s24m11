• Arco Linux is an educational project where they teach you how to install Arch Linux in various different ways depending on how competent you are. It can be as fundamental as command line installation, to as easy as using Calamares installer. They have tutorials for all levels. They also provide pre-completed files and directories for you to take shortcuts in that installation process. So depending on which tutorial you choose to follow, your experience will not be identical to someone else who follows another tutorial. ArcoLinux can be thought of as pure Arch Linux with some branding (branding may be present in the pre-made files if you download them).

• RebornOS started out as a customized version of Antergos when Antergos was still alive. Its basic idea had been to provide options to the user, so there is one ISO that can install any of the 10 desktops which you can choose in the installer. The installer allows you to choose other optional stuff too. It's based on the Cnchi installer used by Antergos. RebornOS is also almost pure Arch Linux, with certain significant AUR packages pre-built for easy installation from the RebornOS repository.

• EndeavourOS was created when Antergos' discontinuation was announced. The moderators grouped together and modified the Calamares installer to give an almost Pure Arch experience with a graphical installer. They're more terminal centric than RebornOS or Garuda.

• Garuda seems to be a riced and modified version of Arch Linux with custom themes and modifications. So it is further away from Arch Linux than the above. It also uses chaotic AUR where AUR packages are pre-built (while RebornOS repository only pre-builds certain selected packages manually and tests them to a certain extent, chaotic AUR is automated and much bigger). Garuda is also more GUI focused than EndeavourOS or RebornOS, with custom graphical tools.

So ArcoLinux is the closest to Arch Linux, followed by EndeavourOS, RebornOS and then Garuda. Being close to Arch Linux may or may not be a good thing depending on what you want to accomplish. Every distro has its own strengths and weaknesses. You can use all of them to discover what is the most suitable for your daily use 🙂
