[D] gabu6113: [reply]: because it's low level, but still offers a lot of high level languages advantages/features. If you want the absolute best performance you've got to use a low level language which means something like C, C++, Rust, or Zig. In the past C++ would probably have been the choice for many, but C++ has suffered from being a kitchen sink where _everything_ has been thrown in over the years and it's a minefield to navigate these days. C (and to a much lesser extent Zig) don't offer as many features as C++ or Rust. They can be good for smaller projects, but past a certain size it will start to get a bit painful to manage. Rust offers the best balance between higher level features, while still maintaining the lower level access (and doing it safely unlike C/C++) necessary to squeeze the most performance out (or in the case of bare metal like embedded and kernels the low level access that's mandatory). All that said, _most_ programs don't actually require those levels of performance or access so you can usually get away with using a higher level language like Python that hides a bunch of the details and manages them for you.
