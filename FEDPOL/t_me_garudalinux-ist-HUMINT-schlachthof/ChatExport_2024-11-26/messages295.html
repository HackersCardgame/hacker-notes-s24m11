[D] back.slash: In windows or mac a bunch of programs just kind of blend together since they're treated as one big chunk under the umbrella of 'operating system', in linux it's more of an obvious chain of programs so you can pick and choose more of the experience. That extends to anything with graphical output from the video card, and the 3 big parts that make up that chain are:

The kernel itself (with video drivers) --> Display Server --> Either Desktop Environment or Window Manager (the former offers more in the way of tiny nice-to-have utilities on top of managing windows, at the cost of more resource use). I'm ignoring a few complicating factors in that chain to simplify it, like any login manager, splash screen, and a lot of stuff that systemd does.

X11 (X Window System) and its newer alternative Wayland both fit the 'Display Server' part of that chain. There is a general push now into using Wayland as default after a long breaking in period, but some bugs that are still a bit messy in Wayland can be worked around by logging in using X to start your desktop environment (KDE, Gnome, etc) instead.
