In reply to this message
Arch is a rolling release and it's inherently less stable, although this is not really a real world problem on a personal machine, people blow this out of proportion.
On a production machine or on a server, I would use something rock solid like Debian or Ubuntu.
On a laptop I like to play with and learn stuff, or on a gaming machine, I wouldn't worry at all and use a rolling release distro like an Arch based one or Gentoo. On this kind of usage the benefits of bleeding edge software outweighs the risk of breaking the system.
And btw, if you're careful with updates, even a rolling released distro can be stable enough. This is not windows, you're not forced to update as soon as updates are out. You can do whatever you want, which includes not updating your system unless it's necessary.
And the whole idea of using BTRFS with Timeshift on Garuda, makes the system even safer. Meaning you can accidentally break it, but you can have consistent backups and restore to a previous state.
