every time you upgrade you should assume the bios will be wiped back to factory defaults, and you should also assume custom profiles will be invalidated. I have had issues caused by restoring past settings between versions much more than a handful of times over years even with different manufacturers. Even when we were just reimporting fan profiles. I advise very very very heavily to play it safe and recreate your profile(s) between versions. I make a list of which settings I change for my friends and customers, so it's not a struggle in the future, you should consider the same.

As for the slow boots, look for a setting called memory context restore, rarely it is shortened to MCR. Turn it ON. It stores last known good memory training data so consecutive boots with the same settings are faster. It will definitely make a difference. Sometimes there's a different one called memory fast train in it's place, likewise if you have that, turn it ON. Sometimes they call it by another poorly chosen name that I can't recall, but it's definitely a choice for every DDR5 platform.
